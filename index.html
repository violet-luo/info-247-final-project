Visualizing US Green Card Applications

17% of the US workforce is foreign born labor


A work visa is a temporary visa that allows foreign workers to come to the United States and work for a specified period of time. However, if a foreign worker wants to work in the U.S. for the long term, they must apply for permanent residency, also known as a Green Card.

The process of obtaining a Green Card can be complex and time-consuming. We are diving into the many factors that can affect the approval rate of green card applications.

Applications come from various types of work visas


The most common work visas are:

  • Foreign Workers with Specialized Skills (H1-B, O1): for foreign workers in specialty occupations that require specialized knowledge or expertise.
  • Treaty Trader Work Visas (E-1): available to individuals who are citizens of countries that have a qualifying treaty with the U.S.
  • Intra-Company Transfer Visas (L-1): allow multinational companies to transfer employees from their foreign offices to their U.S. offices.
  • Business Work Visas (B-1, B-2): for individuals to enter the United States for the purpose of engaging in business-related activities.
  • Temporary Worker Visas (H-2A, H-2B): allow foreign nationals to come to the United States temporarily to work in specific occupations or industries.

The acceptance rates of various work visas differ.
